About me

I began my journey into the world of computers with an obsession with Logo. Seeing what a small turtle and a few mathematical equation could create fascinated and mesmerized me. From that point on I kept teaching myself programming languages and techniques. Today, I have a Masters degree in Human Computer Interaction and Ergonomics and a Bachelors of Engineering in Computers.

I like working in the digital and physical space when it comes to technology. Creating digital artifacts, but I also like to build things with my hands. Give me some cardboard and some masking tape and I can construct an ergonomically designed ATM machine mockup. Hand me a Raspberry pi and and I can make that into a working prototype. Give me an internet connection and web server and I'll have the ATM sending alerts to your phone.

Basically, I like to make things.

My creative process—no matter the medium—is to identify a problem, understand the users, assimilate the sociotechnical context and then iteratively work towards a solution. Continuous improvement through continuous testing is my mantra.

For me, great design is about simple and embodies consistency, context and continuity.
