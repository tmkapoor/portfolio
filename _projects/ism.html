The problem with search is you can't find something you're not looking for. I began by setting 2 research questions; do users understand what the app does? and do users want serendipity in their shopping experience?
I began with a simple user test of the app, which revealed the first problem: users did not understand how the app was suggesting, the selection seemed random. The second thing I did was to conduct surveys and interviews to understand how women (16-24 year old) go about finding items to buy. The other big issue with the app was that the ML was so good that it would suggest items the user already owned. To solve the problem we introduced a very simple splash screen to explain what te app is doing and that teh users need to train the app and secondly, we introduced a 'I own this' tag for items so the app would know what items the user already owns, this also helped the ML algo undersand what items the user had \"commited\" to already, thereby showing high intent." image-set-2: - "https://picsum.photos/300/300?image=1004" - "https://picsum.photos/300/300?image=1005" - "https://picsum.photos/300/300?image=1006" ---
